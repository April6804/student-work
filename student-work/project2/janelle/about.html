Shark Finning

Shark finning is the removal of shark’s fins. Most often the rest of the shark is discarded back into the water alive. They then sink to the bottom, unable to swim, where they either are eaten alive by other fish, bleed to death, or suffocate. Sharks must swim perpetually to keep from sinking to the bottom. Sharks reproduce at a slow rate. Too slow to keep the species thriving at the same rate that they are being killed.

Shark’s fins are in high demand, estimating around $500 per pound of this flavorless cartilage. This makes shark finning is a multi-billion-dollar industry. The rest of the shark’s meat is of very little value and is therefore not worth transporting back to land.

The majority of shark fin is used in a popular dish called shark fin soup. This dish is a delicacy and considered a status symbol in Chinese culture.
